# Persona 1: The Cautious Beginner

**Name:** Sofia (32, marketing manager, Lisbon)
**Capital:** $12K in USDC sitting on Coinbase earning 0.5%
**Experience:** Bought crypto on Coinbase 2 years ago, heard about "DeFi" but never used it
**Risk Tolerance:** Very Low — she's terrified of losing her savings
**Telegram:** Uses daily for personal chats, follows 2 crypto channels

---

## Journey Simulation

### Hero Section
**Sees:** "18,632 yield pools on DeFiLlama. Most of them will lose you money."
**Thinks:** "What's DeFiLlama? What's a yield pool? Will lose me money? This is scary."
**Verdict:** ❌ The hero assumes domain knowledge. Sofia doesn't know what DeFiLlama is, what a yield pool is, or why most would "lose you money." The opening line is fear-inducing without explanation.

**Missing:** A one-sentence plain-English description. "DeFi" is never defined. No anchor for someone who's never used a DeFi protocol.

### The Problem Section
**Sees:** "Finding the good ones means spending hours on aggregators, checking contracts, comparing risk factors..."
**Thinks:** "Aggregators? Checking contracts? I don't know how to do any of that. This tool is for experts."
**Verdict:** ❌ Reinforces that this is a complex, expert-only space. No indication that YieldRadar simplifies this for beginners.

### How It Works
**Sees:** Pipeline: Fetch → Filter → Score → Alert
**Thinks:** "This looks technical. What does 'GoPlus security audit' mean? What's 'impermanent loss'?"
**Verdict:** ⚠️ Jargon-heavy. "Impermanent loss" appears with no explanation. The scoring section references terms a beginner won't understand.

### Risk Scoring Section
**Sees:** 4 tiers with scores 0–100
**Thinks:** "OK, green = safe, red = danger. That's actually helpful. But what does 'Score 75–100' actually mean? How is it calculated?"
**Verdict:** ✅ The color coding and labels are beginner-friendly. ❌ No link to learn more about how the score is calculated. "Low volatility, high TVL, established protocol" — what's TVL?

### Preview / Mockups
**Sees:** Telegram message examples
**Thinks:** "Oh, this comes through Telegram? That's convenient. But wait — what chain is 'Base'? I only know Ethereum."
**Verdict:** ⚠️ Mockups show chain names without context. A beginner doesn't know what Base, Arbitrum, or Solana are.

### Getting Started
**Sees:** 3 steps — Open Telegram, Run /start, Done
**Thinks:** "That seems easy. No wallet? No KYC? That's reassuring actually."
**Verdict:** ✅ This section works well for beginners. Clear, simple, non-threatening.

### Chain Coverage
**Sees:** Ethereum, Base, Solana, Arbitrum, BSC, Polygon
**Thinks:** "I've heard of Ethereum and Solana. The others... I don't know. Will this work for me if I only have Ethereum?"
**Verdict:** ⚠️ No indication of which chain is best for beginners or which is safest. No FAQ.

### Pricing
**Sees:** Free (3 alerts/day), Pro ($9.99/mo), Power ($29.99 coming soon)
**Thinks:** "3 alerts per day? Is that enough? What's an 'alert' exactly? And the Pro plan says 'custom filter rules' — I don't know what to filter."
**Verdict:** ⚠️ Pricing assumes the user knows what an alert is and what filters do. The Free tier value isn't obvious — "3 alerts per day" of what?

### Security Section
**Sees:** Non-custodial, GoPlus, Open source, Self-hostable
**Thinks:** "Non-custodial is good. Open source is good. But 'self-hostable with Docker on a $5 VPS' — what's Docker? What's a VPS?"
**Verdict:** ⚠️ "Self-hostable" description is developer-oriented. Irrelevant to Sofia but makes the page feel technical.

### Roadmap
**Sees:** Shipped, Next up, Planned, Future
**Thinks:** "This looks like a real project with a plan. That's good. But 'LSTM anomaly detection'? 'ERC-4337'? I have no idea what any of this means."
**Verdict:** ⚠️ Roadmap uses heavy jargon. The "Shipped" column is the most reassuring. "Future" column is alienating.

## Key Findings

### Critical Issues (Blocks conversion)
1. **No plain-English intro** — "DeFi" and "yield pool" are never defined for newcomers
2. **Hero is fear-based** — "Most of them will lose you money" is the first thing she sees. No positive framing.
3. **No FAQ section** — Sofia has 20 questions and nowhere to find answers
4. **No social proof** — no testimonials, no user count, no "trusted by X users"

### Moderate Issues (Reduces trust)
5. **TVL, IL, APY jargon** — used without explanation anywhere on the page
6. **Chain coverage section** — doesn't indicate which chain is best/safest for beginners
7. **"Self-hostable" in security section** — irrelevant to beginners, makes product feel technical
8. **Blog is empty** — "Coming soon" undermines the "active project" narrative

### Minor Issues (Polish)
9. **No favicon mention** — minor trust signal
10. **Footer says "MIT License"** — meaningless to non-developers
11. **No explanation of what Telegram Stars are** — pricing says "$9.99/mo" but actual payment is in Stars

### What Works Well
- Getting Started section (3 steps) — clear, simple, reassuring
- Color-coded risk tiers — intuitive even without financial knowledge
- "No wallet needed. No KYC. No sign-up." — strong trust signal for privacy-conscious beginners
- Open source + GitHub link — transparency signal

## Recommendation
Add a "New to DeFi?" explainer section or link. Rewrite the hero to be benefit-first (not fear-first). Add an FAQ. Consider a "Beginner's Guide" blog post as the first piece of content.
